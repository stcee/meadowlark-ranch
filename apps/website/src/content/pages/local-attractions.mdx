## Colorado's Front Range at Your Doorstep

Meadowlark Ranch is situated in one of the most desirable locations in the American West. **Boulder County** and the **Northern Front Range** of Colorado offer visitors an extraordinary combination of outdoor adventure, cultural richness, natural beauty, and the kind of sunshine that makes every day feel like a gift.

With over **300 days of sunshine** annually, a landscape that stretches from the high plains to the Continental Divide, and a community culture that values both active living and thoughtful leisure, this region provides the perfect complement to any visit to the ranch.

## Outdoor Recreation

The geography surrounding Meadowlark Ranch is a playground for outdoor enthusiasts of every kind:

### Hiking & Trail Running

- **The Flatirons** — Boulder's iconic tilted sandstone formations offer world-class hiking just 20 minutes from the ranch, with trails ranging from gentle nature walks to challenging scrambles
- **St. Vrain Greenway** — Miles of paved and natural-surface trail along the St. Vrain Creek, accessible directly from the Longmont area
- **Button Rock Preserve** — A stunning reservoir and canyon hike in the foothills west of Longmont
- **Rabbit Mountain** — Open space with panoramic views of the Front Range and the eastern plains

### Rocky Mountain National Park

Just over an hour from Meadowlark Ranch, **Rocky Mountain National Park** is one of America's most visited and most beloved national parks. With peaks exceeding 14,000 feet, alpine lakes, abundant wildlife, and Trail Ridge Road — the highest continuous paved road in North America — the park offers experiences that range from leisurely scenic drives to backcountry wilderness adventures.

### Cycling

Boulder County is one of the premier cycling destinations in the United States, with:

- **Hundreds of miles of paved bike paths** connecting Longmont, Boulder, and surrounding communities
- **World-class road cycling** on canyon roads and mountain passes
- **Mountain biking trails** at Hall Ranch, Heil Valley Ranch, and throughout the foothills

### Water Sports

- **St. Vrain Creek** — Tubing, kayaking, and fishing through the season
- **Union Reservoir** — Sailing, paddleboarding, and swimming just minutes from the ranch
- **Boulder Reservoir** — A larger body of water offering expanded water sports options

### Winter Activities

- **Skiing and snowboarding** — Eldora Mountain Resort is just 45 minutes from the ranch, with world-class resorts like Winter Park, Copper Mountain, and Arapahoe Basin within a 90-minute drive
- **Cross-country skiing and snowshoeing** in Rocky Mountain National Park and the Indian Peaks Wilderness
- **Ice fishing** at local reservoirs when conditions permit

## Cultural Attractions

### Boulder

Just 20 minutes south of Meadowlark Ranch, the city of **Boulder** offers:

- **Pearl Street Mall** — A vibrant pedestrian mall featuring boutique shopping, galleries, street performers, and some of Colorado's best restaurants
- **University of Colorado campus** — Beautiful Italianate architecture, the Fiske Planetarium, and CU Presents performing arts
- **Boulder Museum of Contemporary Art** — Rotating exhibitions showcasing regional and national artists
- **Chautauqua Park and Auditorium** — A National Historic Landmark offering concerts, lectures, and dining in a stunning foothill setting
- **Boulder Dushanbe Teahouse** — An ornate teahouse hand-crafted in Tajikistan and reassembled in Boulder, serving global cuisine in an extraordinary setting

### Longmont

The city of **Longmont** itself offers a wealth of cultural and entertainment options:

- **Longmont Museum** — Exhibitions exploring the region's history, art, and culture, with a state-of-the-art performance space
- **Longmont Theatre Company** — Live theatrical productions throughout the year
- **First Friday Art Walks** — Monthly gallery walks through Longmont's Creative District
- **Farmers Markets** — Seasonal markets featuring local produce, artisan goods, and community gathering

### Estes Park

The gateway to Rocky Mountain National Park, **Estes Park** is a charming mountain town approximately 45 minutes from the ranch:

- **Historic downtown** with galleries, shops, and restaurants
- **The Stanley Hotel** — The inspiration for Stephen King's *The Shining*, offering tours and fine dining
- **Estes Park Aerial Tramway** — Scenic rides to the summit of Prospect Mountain
- **Wildlife viewing** — Elk, bighorn sheep, and mule deer are commonly spotted in and around town

## Natural Wonders

The landscape surrounding Meadowlark Ranch is defined by geological and natural features that are unique in the world:

- **The Flatiron Mountains** — Boulder's signature geological formation, visible from the ranch, rising dramatically from the plains
- **The Continental Divide** — The backbone of the North American continent, visible on clear days from the ranch property
- **Longs Peak** — At 14,259 feet, the northernmost fourteener in the Colorado Rockies, a landmark visible from across Boulder County
- **St. Vrain Canyon** — A scenic canyon corridor connecting the plains to the mountains, with waterfalls, rock formations, and pristine forests

## 300 Days of Sunshine

Colorado's legendary sunshine is not an exaggeration. The state averages more than 300 days of sunshine per year — more than Miami, San Diego, or Honolulu. This means that regardless of when you visit Meadowlark Ranch, the odds are strongly in favor of blue skies, crisp air, and the kind of light that makes photographers weep with joy.
